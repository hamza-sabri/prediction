Formula DNA — What Every Field Means

Each formula is a JSON object generated by GPT-4 once a month. Here's what every key actually does, where it comes from, and whether it matters.

🤖
All values are GPT-4's judgment based on 10 months of price data + market events
📊

baseline

The foundation — price snapshot + 4 adjustment factors

current_price $1,732.57
fallback only

The price GPT-4 was looking at when it wrote the formula.

⚡ The backend does NOT always use this — it tries to get a fresher price first
1️⃣ First choice: your benchmark data (port-to-port, aggregated via median)
2️⃣ Second choice: master price summary table (ClickHouse query)
3️⃣ Last resort: this current_price value
🛡️ Sanity check: if benchmark price > 3× formula price → fall back to formula price

📈 7.91 means: prices went up ~8% in the last month
🧮 How: (price today − price 4 weeks ago) / price 4 weeks ago × 100
💡 Why it matters: markets have inertia — if prices just jumped, they tend to keep going for a bit

trend_weekly_pct -0.35
dominates long-term

The slow background direction — are prices generally going up or down?

📉 -0.35 means: prices drop 0.35% each week (the minus = going down)
🔭 How GPT-4 gets this: looks at 10 months of history, draws the big-picture line, measures the slope
🧱 It's not about last month (that's momentum) — it's the structural direction of this route
🚧 Capped at ±5% total — so at −0.35%/week it maxes out around week 14
How it accumulates week by week:

W4:−1.40%
W8:−2.80%
W14:−4.90%
W15+:−5.00% (cap)
⚠️ REMOVING
seasonal_factors W01–W52 map

Weekly multipliers meant to capture recurring patterns (CNY, peak season, etc.)

Why it's being removed:
📊 Almost every week is 1.0 — contributing 0% to predictions
🤷 Only W01–W02 (1.01 = +1%) and W51–W52 (0.98 = −2%) have any value at all
🧹 GPT-4 plays it safe and sets everything flat when it doesn't see strong patterns
✅ Safe to remove — nothing will change in the output because it's already doing nothing
⚔️ How Momentum & Trend Fight Each Other
Weeks 1–6: Momentum wins

Momentum is shouting "prices up!" (+6.9%) while trend whispers "prices down" (−0.35%). Momentum overpowers trend. Prices stay elevated.

Weeks 7–8: The turning point

Momentum fades to zero. Events are at half strength. The downward trend starts winning. Prices cross below the base.

Weeks 9–26: Trend dominates

Momentum is gone. Events are fading. Trend is the only voice left, slowly pulling prices down to −5% cap.

🌍

active_events

Real-world disruptions happening RIGHT NOW — confirmed, no confidence score needed

These come from a separate data source of real-world market events. The Airflow pipeline collects them, and GPT-4 reads each one and decides:

computed_impact_pct

"How much does this push prices up or down?" — e.g., Hormuz = +3%

decay_remaining_weeks

"How long until this stops mattering?" — e.g., Hormuz = 8 weeks

risk

Severity rating 1–3. GPT-4's judgment of how serious this is.

forecast

Will this persist (1) or resolve soon (0)?

🔴 Strait of Hormuz tensions +3.0% · 8wk decay
W1W26

Geopolitical — biggest impact, slowest decay. Shipping disruption is real and ongoing.

🟡 CMA CGM surcharge ($4,000/FEU) +1.5% · 6wk decay
W1W26

Commercial — carrier added a surcharge. Directly raises prices but surcharges get removed or absorbed.

🟢 Winter storms — N. Europe ports +1.0% · 4wk decay
W1W26

Weather — temporary disruption. Fades fastest because storms pass. Gone in a few weeks.

Decay formula: impact × e^(−0.693 × week / decay_weeks) — exponential half-life, like radioactive decay
Pattern: Bigger disruptions decay slower. Geopolitical (8wk) > Commercial (6wk) > Weather (4wk). GPT-4 decides these based on the nature of the event.
🔮

forward_signals

Anticipated changes that HAVEN'T fully happened yet — includes a confidence score

Same event pipeline as active_events, but these are future-looking. The key difference: signals have a confidence score because they haven't happened yet.

Effective impact = impact_pct × confidence. So a 1.0% impact with 70% confidence = only 0.7% effective.
📦 Capacity cuts (Ocean Alliance + ONE)
+1.0% × 70% conf = +0.70%

Less supply on the route → higher prices. Plays out over 12 weeks.

📋 French import rules for UK DDP
−0.8% × 80% conf = −0.64%

New regulation may reduce demand or shift trade patterns. The only downward signal. 10-week horizon.

🧧 Pre-CNY front-loading surge
+0.8% × 75% conf = +0.60%

Everyone rushes to ship before Chinese New Year → demand spike → higher prices. Short 6-week horizon.

Decay formula: impact × confidence × e^(−0.693 × week / horizon) — same decay as events but scaled by confidence
Events vs Signals: Events are confirmed (Hormuz IS happening). Signals are anticipated (capacity cuts MIGHT happen). That's why signals need a confidence score and events don't.
🧪

model_metadata

Quality scores — how good was the formula when GPT-4 tested it against past data

backtest_mape_4w / 8w / 12w 5.0

When GPT-4 tested this formula against historical data, it was off by ~5% on average. That's decent. Measured at 4, 8, and 12 weeks out.

backtest_directional_accuracy 0.0 ⚠️

Shows 0% — suspicious. Likely means the backtest didn't run properly for this formula, not that it predicted every direction wrong.

self_adjustment_iterations 0

GPT-4 generated this formula once and shipped it. If > 0, it would mean it went back and refined it.

reasoning keeping ✓

Human-readable explanation of why GPT-4 made these choices. Doesn't affect calculations but essential for understanding decisions later.
